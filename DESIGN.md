# Fieldwork Therapy Design System

## Physical scene

A visitor checks the site on a phone beside a Vienna tram stop after work, with low battery, city noise, and limited patience for medical paperwork.

## Direction

Reference lane: public-wayfinding graphics softened by pencil field notes. The interface uses a route line, numbered trail markers, and cropped city-green shapes without pretending to be wilderness.

## Color strategy

Committed but quiet palette in OKLCH:

- Pollen: `oklch(88% 0.13 91)`
- Aubergine: `oklch(29% 0.07 325)`
- Concrete: `oklch(93% 0.015 112)`
- Leaf ink: `oklch(40% 0.075 145)`
- Alert: `oklch(54% 0.16 28)`

## Typography

Display: Atkinson Hyperlegible Next, for plainspoken confidence and excellent legibility. Body uses the same family with deliberate scale and weight contrast. A handwritten SVG line supplies personality without reducing readability.

## Layout

The route chooser is the hero. Choices are full-width signposts that visibly advance the route. Practical information follows in a staggered two-column map, then a high-contrast contact area.

## Motion

Only route progress, choice hover, and panel transitions move. No ambient floating. Reduced motion switches panels instantly.

## Accessibility

Keyboard-complete radiogroup-like buttons, live result heading, visible back controls, focus restoration, 44px minimum targets, and no reliance on color alone. Emergency information is always reachable in the footer.

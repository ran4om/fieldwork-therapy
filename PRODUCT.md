# Fieldwork Therapy

## Register

Brand landing page with a product-like decision flow.

## Product purpose

Introduce Fieldwork Therapy, a fictional Vienna outdoor psychotherapy practice, and help visitors choose a low-pressure next step. The guided path asks only about format and readiness. It stores and sends nothing.

The fictional founder and lead practitioner is Dr. Amara Weiss.

## Audience

- Adults in Vienna considering psychotherapy but unsure how to begin.
- People interested in walk-and-talk sessions, online sessions, or a calm first conversation.
- Visitors who need practical information before making contact.

## Primary actions

1. Complete the private three-step route chooser.
2. Book or request a 20-minute introductory call.
3. Read practical details, fees, and crisis-service boundaries.

## Ethical constraints

- Never ask for symptoms, diagnosis, trauma, or free-text health information.
- Never persist selections, use analytics on responses, or expose them in URLs.
- State clearly that the tool is not clinical assessment.
- Provide Austrian crisis resources when a visitor indicates immediate support.

## Brand voice

Grounded, clear, companionable. Calm without wellness clichés, forest metaphors, or promises of transformation.

## Strategic principles

- Reduce the burden of deciding what to write in a first email.
- Make cost, location, and cancellation terms visible.
- Outdoor practice is an option, not a claim that nature cures illness.

## Anti-references

- Sage-green wellness templates.
- Stock photos of smiling therapists or mountains.
- Quizzes that imitate diagnosis or collect sensitive answers.

## Content constraints

No testimonials, outcome claims, or fake accreditation. No em dashes in public copy.
